What I do

Running and reporting on search campaigns

I build search campaigns and then read the data — structure, keywords, and ad copy on one side; clickthrough, conversions, and quality scores on the other.

A current focus is Google Ad Grants, the free search-advertising program Google offers eligible nonprofits. I manage the Ad Grants search account of a nonprofit on a volunteer basis. The program comes with strict rules — a 5% clickthrough-rate floor, keyword and quality-score minimums, and conversion-based bidding — so keeping the account healthy means watching the numbers closely.

For that I rely on reporting straight from the Google Ads API, using Google's own official, read-only tooling to pull the metrics that show whether an account is compliant and performing. It reads performance data — it doesn't change campaigns.
