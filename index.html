My work is digital marketing, mainly Google Ads search campaigns — building the account structure, choosing keywords, writing ads, and reading the performance data to keep improving what runs.

A current focus is Google Ad Grants, the free search-advertising program Google offers eligible nonprofits. I manage the Ad Grants search account of a nonprofit on a volunteer basis. The program comes with strict rules — a 5% clickthrough-rate floor, keyword and quality-score minimums, and conversion-based bidding. Keeping an account inside those rules means watching the numbers closely: clickthrough rate, conversions, quality scores, search terms, and impression share.

To do that well I rely on reporting straight from the Google Ads API, using Google's own official, read-only tooling to pull the metrics that show whether an account is healthy and compliant. The reporting is read-only — it reads performance data, it doesn't change campaigns.
